# SIT-HR Acceptable Use Policy

**Last updated: April 2026**
**Version: 1.0**

---

## Purpose

This policy sets out what SIT-HR may and may not be used for. It applies to all users of the platform. Breach of this policy may result in account suspension or termination.

---

## The golden rule

Do not enter personally identifiable information about any individual into SIT-HR.

Use a case reference number - for example CASE-2026-001 - in place of any employee name, job title, or other identifier that could identify a specific individual. This protects both the individual and your organisation.

---

## What you must not enter into the platform

The following must never be entered into SIT-HR under any circumstances:

- Employee names, initials, or nicknames
- National Insurance numbers
- Employee reference numbers used in your own HR system
- Dates of birth
- Home addresses or personal contact details
- Medical diagnoses, conditions, or treatment details
- Disability information
- Pregnancy or maternity information
- Information about an individual's race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, or gender identity
- Financial information including salary details, bank account numbers, or pay references
- Details of ongoing legal proceedings involving named individuals
- Information about criminal convictions or allegations

---

## What SIT-HR is for

SIT-HR is designed for:

- Understanding HR process and employment law obligations in England and Wales
- Navigating absence management, disciplinary procedures, grievance handling, and capability processes
- Generating template HR documents for review and completion
- Understanding how the Acas Code of Practice applies to a given situation
- Understanding statutory obligations including SSP, NLW, Bradford Factor, and the Fair Work Agency framework
- Generating case-referenced export packs for record-keeping

---

## What SIT-HR is not for

SIT-HR must not be used for:

- Obtaining definitive legal advice - always seek qualified legal advice for formal proceedings
- Clinical or occupational health assessments
- Any purpose relating to discrimination, harassment, or unlawful treatment of any individual
- Generating content designed to mislead, coerce, or unfairly disadvantage an employee
- Any purpose outside legitimate employment and HR management

---

## System limitations - read before use

SIT-HR uses System to generate guidance. System can produce outputs that are plausible but incorrect. Employment law is complex and changes regularly.

You must:

- Read all guidance critically before acting on it
- Check that statutory rates, thresholds, and dates cited are current
- Seek qualified legal advice before dismissing an employee, issuing a final written warning, entering a settlement agreement, or taking any action where a protected characteristic or protected disclosure may be relevant
- Review all generated documents for accuracy and completeness before use
- Complete all fields marked INSERT before sending any document to an employee or filing it on record

SIT-HR is a starting point, not a final answer.

---

## Record keeping

Outputs generated by SIT-HR should be treated as working documents requiring review. They are not official HR records until reviewed, completed, and authorised by an appropriate person in your organisation.

Do not file SIT-HR outputs as formal HR records without review.
